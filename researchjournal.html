project 2b: creative coding

scale of the universe

Originally created by Cary and Michael Huang in 2010, Scale of the Universe is an interactive website that allows users to visualise the size of different objects in the universe, from galaxies to quarks. A sequel was created in 2012 with a more polished appearance. The site is interacted with using a scrollbar that the player uses to navigate different size ranges, displaying objects to scale.

I was inspired by this site not only due to the presence of space-related objects (in the form of celestial bodies being compared), but because the use of a simple interactive element allowed for hours of exploration on the site.

library of babel

Library of Babel is a website created by Jonathan Basile in 2015, based on the short story "The Library of Babel" by Jorge Luis Borges. Similar to the titular library, the website contains every possible permutation of letters and punctuation within a 3200 character limit. Like in the "infinite monkeys with typewriters" thought experiment, some of these randomly generated pages will have legible words by sheer chance. Basile calls this website an example of "iterature", a combination of literature and iterability.

I found this site interesting as it fit into the "nonsense" theme of this subject. The idea that meaningful phrases created by chance exist in the site, waiting for a visitor to stumble upon it, is a creative application of a thought experiment in the form of a website.
